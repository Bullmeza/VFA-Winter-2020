Hanukkah Dance

This piece, performed by the students from gr. 9-12, is called “Hanukkah Dance.” It was arranged by Stephen Bulla and this rhythmic piece is based on a traditional melody sung at Hanukkah celebrating a child’s perspective on the “Festival of Lights.” It is a light and joyful dance written in 4/4 time and presents a great opportunity to teach balanced ensemble playing in this style of music.

Hockey Night in Canada Theme

The students in gr. 9-12 will now perform a rendition of a true winter classic: the “Hockey Night in Canada Theme.” It is often referred to as Canada’s 2nd “National Anthem.” Composed by Deloras Claman, this arrangement by Howard Cable captures all of the fun and excitement of the original recording. A powerful march, with a cool swing bridge, performers enjoy a study of syncopated rhythm; expressive dynamics; building harmonies; and powerful chord structures. Let’s play some puck!!

Rockin’ Around the Christmas Tree

This piece, performed by the students from gr. 9-12, is a John Tatgenhorst arrangement of Johnny Marks’ “Rockin’ Around the Christmas Tree.” You may have heard this rendition of the song by American singer Brenda Lee in the 1950s, as well as in a particular scene in the film “Home Alone” when Kevin McAllister initially tries to fool those “Wet Bandits” by showing that his family was actually home before setting off those booby traps. This holiday pop tune is a favourite year after year and can now be a part of your seasonal festivities. Skillfully arranged for a band in a moderate rock style, this 1950s Christmas classic will be the hit of your holiday concert.
